# RFC-0001: Project Vision

**Status:** Accepted  
**Author:** Project contributors  
**Date:** 2026-07-13

---

## Summary

TWW3 Companion is an open-source application that helps Total War: Warhammer III players **organise, understand, and maintain** large mod collections through structured knowledge — not by replacing existing mod management tools.

---

## Problem Statement

Warhammer III modding supports deep customisation: faction overhauls, UI mods, balance packs, framework dependencies, and compatibility patches. Players who run serious collections face recurring pain:

- **Volume** — Hundreds of workshop items are difficult to track in notes or spreadsheets alone.
- **Context loss** — Why a mod was added, what it conflicts with, and what it depends on are forgotten over time.
- **Fragmentation** — Information lives in Steam comments, Discord threads, personal markdown files, and memory.
- **False expectations** — General-purpose mod managers excel at install and load order; they are weaker at long-term *documentation* of a collection as a knowledge artefact.

The project originated from reviewing a large personal mod collection and recognising that the unmet need was **knowledge management**: a durable, searchable record of what the collection is, how it fits together, and how healthy it is — separate from launching the game or rewriting load order.

---

## Goals

1. **Import** mod information from practical sources (markdown lists, Workshop IDs).
2. **Persist** collections locally in open, documented formats.
3. **Organise** with categories, tags, and notes suited to how players think about their lists.
4. **Search** quickly across the collection.
5. **Relate** mods through dependencies and compatibility annotations.
6. **Assess** collection health with a transparent, explainable score — not a black box.
7. **Export** knowledge for backup, sharing, and migration.
8. Remain **offline-first** and **privacy-respecting** (player-owned data by default).

---

## Non-Goals

TWW3 Companion will **not**:

- Replace Steam Workshop, the game launcher, or dedicated mod managers (RPFM, Kaedrin's, WH3 Mod Manager, etc.).
- Download, install, enable, or disable mods in the game.
- Automatically resolve load-order conflicts or merge mods.
- Patch game files or distribute copyrighted mod content.
- Require cloud accounts or upload collections to project servers by default.
- Position itself as a cheat, exploit, or multiplayer circumvention tool.

Stating non-goals clearly protects contributors and users from scope creep and sets honest expectations.

---

## Philosophy

| Principle | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| **Knowledge over management** | Document and understand; do not pretend to control the game install. |
| **Player-owned data** | Files should be portable, inspectable, and under the user's control. |
| **Honest scope** | Integrate with the ecosystem; do not duplicate specialised tools poorly. |
| **Explainability** | Health scores and warnings should be traceable to recorded facts, not opaque heuristics alone. |
| **Incremental delivery** | Ship useful slices (import → organise → relate) rather than waiting for a monolith. |
| **Living design** | Vision docs evolve; major changes go through RFCs and [DECISIONS.md](../DECISIONS.md). |

---

## Success Criteria

The project vision is achieved when:

1. **A player with a large collection** can import their mod list, annotate it, and find any mod or note in seconds without relying on external spreadsheets.
2. **Dependencies and compatibility** recorded in the app reduce surprise breakages after game or mod updates (because knowledge was captured, not because the app auto-fixed anything).
3. **Health score** gives a quick, understandable signal backed by documented criteria (missing dependencies, known conflicts, stale notes, etc.).
4. **Export/import round-trip** preserves collection knowledge across machines and versions.
5. **Contributors and users** can read [README.md](../README.md) and this RFC and agree on what the product is and is not.
6. **v1.0** is trusted for everyday maintenance of a Warhammer III mod collection without replacing workshop or launcher workflows.
